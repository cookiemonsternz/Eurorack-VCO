# VCO Journal 3 - 18/04/2026

Hello again!

I lied yesterday a bit, I've got a few more changes I'd like to make to the circuit that I've been thinking about overnight. Namely...
- Clean it up a bit (get rid of redundant buffering / sections)
- Move the pwm generation from the triangle wave to the sawtooth wave (triangle is messy)
- Try and fix the triangle wave
- Get rid of sawtooth offset

---

First of all, I figured out that the output high pass filter for my sawtooth wave was set to 0.15Hz, which is quite low, so increasing this to ~15-20Hz should help remove any dc offset.

And yep now its a very nice -6V to 6V
![Sawtooth w/o dc offset](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d9dde-3bf1-7d19-8d6b-bbbd44adebfa/paste-1776470079286.png)

---


### Triangle stuff!

I made a couple tweaks from some comments I found online, and adding a small capacitor over the feedback resistor and adding a trimpot in series very much helps (trimpot will only matter irl so not included here).

![Better triangle](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d9ebf-5bf3-7536-b069-27d4feed8f09/paste-1776484833033.png)
Much much much cleaner, also the tidy up I did to the sawtooth meant that I could get rid of the bias tweaking and just chuck it straight to ground.

Also changed around the filtering and scaling and now it's a pretty clean 10v p2p at gnd (not in the screenshot though cos I hadn't done that yet).

---

### PWM on sawtooth + tidy up.

I've not only swapped it over to run off the sawtooth, but also rejiggerated the cv processing a wee bit. It's now in the proper +-5V range, and theres a trimpot for adjustment (and also so that its just a square wave when the cv is disconnected).

![PWM](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d9ed0-56af-7b0f-8fc4-6f924ef92abf/paste-1776485945842.png)

---

### Cleaner triangle also means cleaner sine wave!
Now with proper output scaling and impedance :)
![Sing](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d9ed4-0dbe-7693-bc6e-b0e34a56a12e/paste-1776486189310.png)

I've also decided to get rid of the flip flop square wave. It can just be emulated with the pwm output (albeit a tiny bit less cleanly) but the extra component cost (an uncommon-ish one as well) probably isn't worth it.

I've also went around and made sure all of the outputs have the correct impedance :)

And with that, the VCO design is officially complete!
